
# Strategic Systems Map

## The Architecture of Strategic Systems Thinking

All complex systems can be understood through five layers.

OUTCOMES
↓
STRATEGIC PATTERNS
↓
SYSTEM ARCHETYPES
↓
CAUSAL MECHANISMS
↓
SYSTEM STRUCTURE

Strategists diagnose systems by moving from visible behavior to underlying structure.

---

## Layer 1 — Observable Outcomes

These are visible system behaviors.

Examples:

- Product development slowing
- Traffic congestion
- Platform dominance
- Price wars
- Infrastructure collapse

Important principle:

Events are symptoms. Structure is the cause.

---

## Layer 2 — Strategic Patterns

Strategic patterns are macro-level dynamics that appear across industries.

Examples:

- Coordination Trap
- Price War Spiral
- Platform Flywheel
- Infrastructure Lag
- Innovation Blindness

These patterns often map to system archetypes.

---

## Layer 3 — System Archetypes

Archetypes are recurring system structures.

Examples:

- Limits to Growth
- Success to the Successful
- Tragedy of the Commons
- Escalation
- Shifting the Burden
- Growth and Underinvestment

Archetypes emerge from combinations of mechanisms.

Example:

Growth → Coordination Complexity → Decision Bottleneck → Slower Execution

Archetype: Limits to Growth

---

## Layer 4 — Causal Mechanisms

Mechanisms are the forces driving system behavior.

Examples:

- Growth
- Coordination Complexity
- Decision Bottleneck
- Network Effects
- Infrastructure Lag
- Structural Inertia
- Risk Aversion

Mechanisms combine to form archetypes.

---

## Layer 5 — System Structure

Structure determines how systems behave.

Examples:

- Organizational design
- Infrastructure capacity
- Incentive systems
- Governance rules
- Resource allocation

Strategic intervention occurs by changing system structure.

---

## Leverage Points

Strategic intervention occurs at leverage points.

Examples:

- Parameters (pricing)
- Structure (organizational design)
- Information flows (transparency)
- Rules (incentives)
- System goals
- Paradigms

Deeper leverage points create larger system impact.

---

## Strategic Diagnosis Loop

Outcome
↓
Strategic Pattern
↓
Archetype
↓
Mechanism Chain
↓
Structural Constraint
↓
Leverage Point
↓
Strategic Intervention

Example:

Outcome: Product delivery slowing

Pattern: Coordination Trap

Archetype: Limits to Growth

Mechanisms: Growth → Coordination Complexity → Decision Bottleneck

Leverage Point: Organizational structure

Intervention: Decentralize decision authority
