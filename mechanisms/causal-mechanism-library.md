
# Causal Mechanism Library

Causal mechanisms are the **building blocks of system behavior**.

Below are examples of important mechanisms used in the framework.

## Growth

Expansion of system scale or activity.

Example:

Startup growth → team expansion → coordination complexity.

## Coordination Complexity

Communication overhead increases rapidly as the number of actors increases.

## Decision Bottleneck

Centralized decision authority slows execution.

## Network Effects

More participants increase value for all participants.

## Infrastructure Lag

Demand grows faster than capacity expansion.

## Resource Saturation

Systems approach capacity limits.

## Risk Aversion

Failures lead organizations to add control mechanisms.

## Structural Inertia

Past success creates resistance to change.

## Knowledge Concentration

Critical system knowledge is concentrated in a few actors.

## Capability Trap

Short‑term solutions prevent long‑term capability building.

These mechanisms combine to produce **system archetypes**.
