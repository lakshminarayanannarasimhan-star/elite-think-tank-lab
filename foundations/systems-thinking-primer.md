
# Systems Thinking Primer

Systems thinking studies how **system structures produce outcomes**.

Traditional problem solving focuses on events.

Example:

A product release is delayed.

Event‑based thinking asks:

What mistake caused the delay?

Systems thinking asks:

What structural forces produced the delay?

Example mechanism chain:

Growth  
→ Team expansion  
→ Coordination complexity  
→ Decision bottleneck  
→ Slower execution  

This pattern is known as the **Limits to Growth archetype**.

Key principles:

1. Systems produce outcomes through structure.
2. Feedback loops drive system evolution.
3. Constraints limit growth.
4. Strategic intervention requires structural understanding.

Understanding systems thinking allows analysts to diagnose complex environments such as organizations, markets, and ecosystems.
